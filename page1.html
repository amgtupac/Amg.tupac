History


The Regional Maritime University (RMU), located in Accra, Ghana, has its roots in a cooperative initiative by West African countries to address the demand for trained personnel in the maritime sector. The history of RMU dates back to 1958, when the Nautical College of Ghana was established to train seafarers for the maritime industry.

Start Now

Early Beginnings (1958)

The Nautical College of Ghana was founded by the government of Ghana with a focus on maritime training, primarily for seafarers. The institution's objective was to equip young men with the skills needed to support Ghana’s maritime operations. It began offering basic courses in navigation, seamanship, and related fields. 

Transition to a Regional Institution (1971)

In 1971, the idea of a regional approach to maritime training led to the transformation of the Nautical College into the Regional Maritime Academy (RMA). This initiative was spearheaded by the United Nations Economic Commission for Africa (UNECA) and the International Maritime Organization (IMO) in cooperation with five West African countries: Ghana, The Gambia, Sierra Leone, Liberia, and Cameroon. The aim was to establish a specialized institution to provide high-quality training for maritime professionals in the sub-region.

The Regional Maritime Academy thus expanded its programs to include courses in marine engineering, marine electricals, and other specialized maritime fields. This regional status helped improve cooperation among member states in providing skilled manpower for their respective maritime industries. 


Achieving University Status (2007)

The Regional Maritime Academy officially became the Regional Maritime University (RMU) in 2007. This transition was a result of its growth in infrastructure, academic programs, and regional influence. RMU gained university status, enabling it to award its degrees and diplomas. This shift marked a new chapter in the institution’s history, focusing not only on seafarer training but also on broader maritime and logistics studies, business, and engineering programs.

The RMU’s expansion reflected its growing influence and commitment to addressing the changing needs of the maritime industry. It continued to attract students from across West Africa and beyond, training professionals in a wide range of disciplines, including port and shipping management, environmental science, and fisheries.

Present Day

Today, the RMU remains a leading institution in maritime education and training in West Africa. It is recognized by international maritime bodies, including the IMO, and continues to offer programs in shipping, logistics, port management, marine engineering, and environmental studies, among others. The university also plays a key role in supporting sustainable development in the maritime industry, providing research, technical assistance, and skilled graduates to enhance the region’s maritime sector.

The RMU’s long history of dedication to maritime education has made it a cornerstone in the development of West Africa’s maritime capabilities, contributing significantly to regional growth and international maritime operations.
